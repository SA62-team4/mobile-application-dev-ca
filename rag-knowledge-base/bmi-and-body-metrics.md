# BMI And Body Metrics

Source note: team-curated educational summary for a student wellness app, informed by public health BMI screening guidance.

This app provides general wellness education and does not provide medical diagnosis or treatment.

## Quick Guidance

BMI, body mass index, body metrics, height, weight, weight trend, and healthy weight questions should be answered as screening and habit-support topics, not as diagnosis. BMI is calculated from weight and height: BMI = weight in kilograms divided by height in metres squared. In this app, height comes from the profile and weight comes from recent wellness records.

For adults, common BMI screening categories are: under 18.5 is underweight, 18.5 to less than 25 is healthy weight, 25 to less than 30 is overweight, and 30 or higher is obesity. BMI is only a screening measure. It does not directly measure body fat, muscle, bone mass, fitness, health behaviours, blood pressure, cholesterol, or medical history.

## Interpreting A BMI Trend

A BMI trend can help users notice broad changes over time, especially when the same height and similar weighing routine are used. A single weight or BMI entry should not be overinterpreted because daily weight can shift with hydration, meals, salt intake, exercise, menstrual cycle, and normal body variation. Weekly or multi-week trends are usually more meaningful than one day.

## Habit-Support Response Style

If a user asks, "Is my BMI good?", the app should explain the category cautiously, mention that BMI is a screening number, and suggest sustainable habits: regular meals, hydration, movement, sleep, stress management, and consistent tracking. The app should avoid body shaming, appearance judgements, crash diets, calorie prescriptions, or rapid weight-loss claims.

## When To Seek Professional Advice

Users should consider professional advice if they have unexplained weight change, very low or very high BMI, dizziness, fainting, eating concerns, a history of disordered eating, pregnancy, chronic illness, or worries about weight-related health risks. The app should encourage a health professional to interpret BMI alongside other health information.
