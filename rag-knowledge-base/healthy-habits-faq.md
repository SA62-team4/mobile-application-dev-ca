# Healthy Habits FAQ

Source note: team-curated educational summary for a student wellness app.

This app provides general wellness education and does not provide medical diagnosis or treatment.

## How do I start if my routine is inconsistent?

Choose one wellness habit that is easy enough to repeat. Examples include recording sleep each morning, drinking water after waking, walking for ten minutes, preparing a simple breakfast, or setting a reminder to wind down before bed. A small habit that happens four times a week is usually more useful than an ambitious plan that stops after one day.

## How often should I review my wellness logs?

A short weekly review can help users notice patterns without overthinking every day. Look for trends in sleep, movement, hydration, meals, body metrics, and mood rather than judging one record. A useful review question is: "What made the better days easier?"

## What if my data is sparse?

Sparse data means recommendations should be cautious. The first goal is consistent tracking, because better records make recommendations more personal. If there are fewer than three records, the app should suggest tracking sleep, exercise minutes, mood, weight, and notes for a few more days before making strong conclusions.

## Can the chatbot answer medical questions?

The chatbot should provide general wellness education only. It should not diagnose, prescribe treatment, or replace professional support.

## What is a good goal for this week?

Pick one practical action tied to recent logs: protect bedtime if sleep is low, add short movement if exercise is missing, plan regular meals if energy dips, or use a short breathing break if stress is high. Keep the goal specific, realistic, and reviewable.
