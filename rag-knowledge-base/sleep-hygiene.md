# Sleep Hygiene Basics

Source note: team-curated educational summary for a student wellness app.

This app provides general wellness education and does not provide medical diagnosis or treatment.

Healthy sleep habits often begin with consistency. A regular bedtime and wake time can help the body anticipate rest. A calming wind-down routine, such as stretching, reading, or quiet breathing, can make it easier to transition from study or work into sleep.

Screens, bright light, caffeine, and intense late-night activity may make sleep harder for some people. Evening exercise can still be helpful, but users who notice poorer sleep after intense workouts may try lighter activity or finish exercise earlier.

Small changes are easier to sustain than dramatic routines. A user who sleeps fewer than seven hours can start by protecting a realistic bedtime for three nights and reviewing whether their energy improves.
