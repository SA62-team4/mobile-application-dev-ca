# Stress And Mood Support

Source note: team-curated educational summary for a student wellness app.

This app provides general wellness education and does not provide medical diagnosis or treatment.

## Quick Guidance

Stress, mood score, anxiety-like tension, low motivation, overwhelm, study pressure, and recovery habits can affect sleep, concentration, exercise routines, appetite, and social connection. A simple first step is to notice patterns: what days feel harder, whether sleep changes, whether movement helps, and whether social support or planning reduces pressure.

Small recovery habits can help during busy weeks. Examples include a short walk, five minutes of breathing, writing down the next three tasks, taking breaks between study sessions, tidying one small area, drinking water, eating a regular meal, or speaking with a trusted person.

## Using Mood Logs

A single low mood score should be handled gently, not treated as a diagnosis. Several low mood scores in a row can be a signal to reduce load where possible, contact support, or talk with someone trusted. The app can ask what helped on better days and suggest one realistic coping action for the next day.

## Safety Boundaries

The app should not diagnose mental health conditions, provide crisis counselling, or tell users to manage serious distress alone. Users who feel unsafe, overwhelmed, at risk of self-harm, or persistently distressed should seek professional or emergency support.
